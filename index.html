Activism

From 2015, I started to be a volunteer for Mozilla. Since when Firefox was born I used it and Install it in all Computer I used or repaired, and, when I knowed the existence of the FirefoxStudentAmbassador(FSA) Program in Mozilla I wanted to be involved. After 1 year where I partecipate at various campaign promoted by Mozilla, I haved the opportunity to been selected how Regional Ambassador Lead (RAL) for my Nation. I was be the first RAL selected in all the Europe. For me this was be awesome. In the last five months all togheter we are worked hard to create an more impact program and the results given for me are really fantastic. In only five months we have planned some events and increased the number of active volunteers in the program and in the Italian Mozilla Community (Mozilla Italia). during this experience, I wanted to create an Local community, here in Cagliari, focused to help the people to increase their skills concerning the Web Literacy. With this focus and this goals I created the Mozilla Cagliari Club, a inclusive Club where all people can partecipate to help or learn how use, make and partecipate on the web. After the experience as RAL of the FSA program I was be selected as CampusAdvisoryCommittee Member and Regional Coordinator for the Mozilla Campus Club program. A new exciting program for university students that have the goals to create open and collaborative clubs where the students can build and learn in opensource technologies in different ways. Six months ago, I've been selected as Reps Regional Coach. This role have the goals to helps the various National Communities in the World to identify the best way to increase the partecipation at all their community activities. At the moment I'm involved in the Open Leadership Training Program as Mentor. This program is oriented to help the people to get all the instruments helpful to be a good open source project leader. I'm excited to be involved as mentor and I'm happy to help other in the world to create community who works in opensource. I hope to update soon this section with some other awesome news, but, the more important thing is that I'm proud to be a part of the fantastic Mozilla family.
